The Substance Abuse & Mental Health Services Administration conducts a survey of over 50,000 Americans on their drinking and drug use behavior each year.



These slides summarize drinking statistics by age group based on results of the 2012 National Survey on Drug Use and Health.

When Adolescents & Young Adults Drink, They Drink A Lot

The percent of Americans who report drinking in the past 30 days increases from about 2% for 12-13 Year Olds to over 90% for 21-25 Year Olds before starting to decline.

Among the younger age groups in particular, when they drink, they tend to drink a lot. For example, a typical 18-20 year old male is drinking an average of 5.3 drinks every time he drinks.

Binge Drinking is a Major Concern

Binge drinking is defined as drinking 5 or more drinks during one drinking occasion. The percent of the population who are binge drinking is alarmingly high, with over half (53%) of 21-25 year old males reporting binge drinking at least once in the past 30 days.

And those who binge at all are binging a lot. For example, males between 16 and 20 years old who binged at all drank 5 or more drinks two-thirds of the times they drank anything!
